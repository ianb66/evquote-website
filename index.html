September 2025

UK EV Market Reaches Record 26.5% Share

Despite August being traditionally a low-volume month, electric vehicles achieved their highest ever market share, demonstrating the accelerating shift towards sustainable transport.

The UK's electric vehicle market has reached a historic milestone, with EVs capturing 26.5% of all new car sales in August 2025. This achievement is particularly remarkable given that August is traditionally one of the lowest-volume months for car sales in the UK.

The Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders (SMMT) reported that 45,281 new electric vehicles were registered in August, representing a 35% increase compared to the same month in 2024. This surge demonstrates the growing consumer confidence in electric mobility and the expanding availability of EV models across all price segments.

Key factors driving this growth include:

  • Improved charging infrastructure with over 1 million public charging points now available
  • Extended government grants making EVs more affordable for consumers
  • New model launches from major manufacturers offering greater choice
  • Rising fuel costs making electric vehicles more economically attractive

Industry experts predict that the UK is on track to exceed its target of 80% EV sales by 2030, with some forecasting this milestone could be reached as early as 2028. The government's commitment to banning new petrol and diesel car sales by 2030 continues to drive both consumer adoption and manufacturer investment in electric technology.

August 2025

Government Expands Electric Car Grant Scheme

New funding announced to support EV adoption across all income levels, with enhanced grants for home charging installations.

The UK government has announced a significant expansion of its electric vehicle grant schemes, allocating an additional £200 million to support EV adoption across all income brackets. The enhanced program aims to make electric vehicles accessible to a broader range of consumers, particularly targeting middle and lower-income households.

The expanded grant scheme includes:

  • Enhanced OZEV Grants: Home charging installation grants increased from £350 to £500
  • New EV Purchase Support: Up to £3,000 grants for households earning under £50,000 annually
  • Workplace Charging Expansion: WCS grants doubled to support business EV infrastructure
  • Rural Area Support: Additional £1,000 grants for properties in rural locations

Transport Secretary announced: "This expansion represents our commitment to ensuring the electric revolution benefits everyone, not just those who can afford the initial investment. By 2030, we want electric vehicles to be the obvious choice for all UK drivers."

The grants are available immediately and can be combined with manufacturer incentives, potentially reducing the cost of EV ownership by up to £5,000. Applications can be made through approved installers and dealerships nationwide.

July 2025

Major Charging Network Expansion Announced

Leading providers commit to doubling charging points by 2026, addressing range anxiety concerns for potential EV buyers.

A consortium of the UK's leading charging network operators has announced the largest expansion of public EV charging infrastructure in British history. The £2.5 billion investment will see the number of public charging points double from 1 million to 2 million by the end of 2026.

The expansion focuses on three key areas:

  • Rapid Charging Hubs: 500 new ultra-rapid charging stations along major motorways
  • Urban Charging: 300,000 new charging points in city centers and residential areas
  • Destination Charging: 200,000 chargers at shopping centers, workplaces, and leisure facilities

The project, supported by government funding and private investment, addresses the primary concern of potential EV buyers: range anxiety. Recent surveys show that 68% of consumers cite charging availability as their main barrier to EV adoption.

Key features of the new network include:

  • 350kW ultra-rapid chargers capable of adding 200 miles of range in 10 minutes
  • Universal payment systems accepting all major cards and mobile payments
  • Real-time availability tracking through smartphone apps
  • Renewable energy sourcing for all new charging points

The first phase of installations begins in September 2025, with completion expected by December 2026. This expansion positions the UK as having one of the most comprehensive charging networks globally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about EV charger installation and our services.

How much does it cost to install an EV charger at home?

Home EV charger installation typically costs between £800-£1,500, depending on your property and charger type. The government OZEV grant provides up to £350 towards the cost, making it more affordable. We'll provide you with detailed quotes from certified installers in your area.

How long does installation take?

Most home EV charger installations take 2-4 hours to complete. This includes a site survey, electrical work, mounting the charger, and testing. Our installers will provide a specific timeframe based on your property's requirements during the initial consultation.

Do I need planning permission for an EV charger?

In most cases, no planning permission is required for home EV chargers as they fall under permitted development rights. However, there may be restrictions for listed buildings or conservation areas. Our installers will advise you on any requirements specific to your property.

What's the difference between 7kW and 22kW chargers?

A 7kW charger is suitable for most homes and provides a full charge overnight (6-8 hours). A 22kW charger is faster (2-4 hours) but requires a three-phase electricity supply, which most UK homes don't have. We'll assess your property and recommend the best option for your needs.

Are your installers certified?

Yes, all installers in our network are MCS (Microgeneration Certification Scheme) certified and fully qualified electricians. This ensures high-quality installation and makes you eligible for government grants. We also provide a 2-year warranty on all installations.

Can I charge any electric vehicle with a home charger?

Yes, all modern EV chargers use the universal Type 2 connector (Mode 3), which is compatible with all electric vehicles sold in the UK. This includes Tesla, BMW, Audi, Nissan, and all other EV brands. The charger will work with any current or future EV you purchase.

What happens if something goes wrong after installation?

All our installations come with a comprehensive 2-year warranty covering both the charger and installation work. We also provide 24/7 customer support. If you experience any issues, simply contact us and we'll arrange for a certified technician to resolve the problem quickly.

How do I get started?

Simply fill out our quote form above, and we'll connect you with up to 3 certified installers in your area. They'll provide free, no-obligation quotes and can usually schedule a site survey within 48 hours. The entire process from quote to installation typically takes 1-2 weeks.
